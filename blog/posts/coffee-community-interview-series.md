**Introducing "Coffee & Community": An Interview Series by the founders of joe**

Welcome to "Coffee & Community," the interview series where we explore the vibrant world of coffee through the eyes of those who make it happen. In this series, the founders of joe interview coffee shop owners and roasters from around the U.S. to share their stories, lessons learned, and tips with peers.

## Featuring Vanessa Alvarez

In our very first interview, we are thrilled to feature Vanessa Alvarez, the owner of Catalina's Coffee & Cocktails in Sunnyside, Washington. Vanessa's journey from the corporate world to owning a coffee shop is nothing short of inspiring.

With a background that includes roles at the University of Washington and Redfin, Vanessa has always been passionate about creating inclusive environments. This value is a cornerstone of her business philosophy at Catalina's Coffee & Cocktails.

During the interview, Vanessa opens up about the challenges she faced transitioning from a structured corporate environment to the dynamic world of coffee entrepreneurship. She shares valuable insights on integrating personal values into business practices and the importance of fostering inclusivity in the workplace.

## Key Takeaways

- **The Power of Community**: Vanessa emphasizes the significance of building a community within and around your coffee shop. This sense of belonging not only attracts customers but also creates a supportive network for business growth.

- **Balancing Profit and Purpose**: She discusses the delicate balance of maintaining profitability while staying true to your core values. For Vanessa, creating a welcoming and inclusive space is just as important as the bottom line.

- **Learning from Challenges**: Transitioning from a corporate job to owning a coffee shop came with its own set of challenges. Vanessa shares how she navigated these obstacles and what she learned along the way.
